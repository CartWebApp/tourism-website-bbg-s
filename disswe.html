Valborg

Valborg, also known as Walpurgis, is a Swedish tradition that is similar to halloween. During Valborg, bonfires are lit to keep away ghouls, demons and witches. Valborg is also on the birthday of the current king of Sweden, King Carl XVI Gustav, and so therefore is another holiday for Swedes. Valborg occurs on April 30th and happens all over Sweden. Valborg is a public event, and it’s somewhat similar to Burning Man in America. It’s a large festival that ends off with a large piece of wood being burned and fireworks being shot for a large celebration.

Christmas Markets

The Swedes also love Christmas. The Christmas markets in Sweden are special because of all of the unique items that people are able to purchase. From pelts, to fine smithing, the Christmas markets in Sweden really have an abundant amount of items for those who are interested. The markets are actually all over Scandinavia; so, finding certain items can take time. The Christmas markets happen all over Sweden, but dates vary on when they do occur. Visitors can taste all different kinds of foods there too like surströmming, cheese, different varieties of bread, and pastries. There are special and unique attractions such as a ferry ride to a Swedish arts and crafts museum. In Sweden, the markets are a different experience due to the abundance of culture in each part of Sweden.
